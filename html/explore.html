Sports Complex

Siri Fort Sports Complex has been set up by the Delhi Development Authority as a part of development and growth of Sports in Delhi. The complex was built in 1989, next to the Asian Games village by the Delhi Development Authority (DDA), which also runs the facility now. Also close by is the Siri Fort Auditorium complex. It has various sporting facilities like flood lit Tennis, Basketball, Golf Driving Range, an Indoor Badminton Stadium and playing facilities for Squash, Table Tennis, Billiards, Cricket, Football, Hockey, Aerobics, Yoga, Taekwondo, Multi gym, Swimming, Mini Golf Course, Jogging, Croquet, Skating, Shooting , Reiki, Naturopathy & Children Park. Facilities like Kruv Maga, Western Dance, Balle and Jazz have recently been added.

With its strategic location in the heart of South Delhi and it's well equipped facilities in 27 sporting disciplines, the complex also attracts for it's sheer aesthetic value. Encompassed by lush green lawns, the complex houses clay and deco turf courts for tennis enthusiasts, a plethora of other sporting facilities like cricket, hockey, basketball, a full service fitness centre, and even yoga and aerobics for the health conscious. In addition, the complex is home to the most popular Golf driving range of the country and an air rifle/pistol shooting range. The newly constructed aquatic pool is an added attraction. It's modern squash courts, Madan Lal Academy for Cricket, coaching for Tennis, Badminton and Squash remains star attractions.

F.A.Q

Q1. How many sports complexes have been developed by DDA ?

Ans. Fifteen Sports Complexes have been developed , five in South Delhi, four in East Delhi, three each in North Delhi and West Delhi.Details of Sports Complexes, Mini Sports Complexes, Swimming Pools, Gymnasiums, and Golf Courses developed by DDA are given under Infrastructure on this Website.

Q2. Where exactly are these sports complexes located ?

Ans. Please see map as given in website. You may also contact the complex on Telephone Nos. as given below. However, it is advisable to visit the complex nearest to your residence and see for yourself after getting the directions from the Reception of the complex.

Q3. What are the facilities available in each of these complex ?

Ans. Details available on website. New facilities and upgradation planned is also given there under sports complex.

Q4. Do you have membership in these complexes ? How can one become a member ?

Ans. Yes, various types of memberships are availaible. Membership is approved by or on behalf of the Sports Management Board. Membership , however, only provides playing rights in the complexes. Details of types of memberships and the rates of entry fee (non-refundable) and monthly subscription are given at Table-II. One need not be enrolled as a member to use the facilities of the complex. One can become a casual member for a day by paying Rs. 40/- per person. This system is called "Pay & Play" and can be availed by anyone.

Q5. Are there any other special schemes for coaching ?

Ans. Yes, DDA has started the following schemes details of which are covered in the Sports Wing, brief on website. - Talent Search Schemes in Tennis & Cricket. - DDA Athletics Fellowship Scheme. - DDA Football Promotion Scheme.

Q6. Are any coaching facilities available?

Ans. Coaching is conducted in various disciplines such as Cricket, Tennis, Skating, Karate, Aerobics, Yoga etc in our complexes. It is best to visit the complex of your choice and obtain information available regarding the coach, rates, timings etc.These are also availaible on the website of each complex.
